Is Tom Basden Married Or Is He Still Dating His Partner?

Before we talk about Tom’s personal life, we must talk about what he has achieved in the entertainment industry.

Born in Surrey, London, Tom received his education at King’s College School and Pembroke College. Tom started his career with a one-man show at the 2007 Edinburgh Festival Fringe, where he also won Edinburgh Comedy Awards under the “Best Newcomer” category. In the same year, Tom’s short film,  The One and Only Herb McGwyer Plays Wallis Island, won UK Film Council Kodak Award for Best British Short Film.

Some of Tom’s acting credits include The Last Bus, After Life, Mandy, Plebs (also wrote and produced), Quacks, W1A, The Wrong Mans, The Armstrong and Miller Show, and Star Stories, among others.

As a writer, he has written Fresh Meat, a British comedy-drama TV series, which gave him a nomination for the 2012 BAFTA Craft Awards in the Break-Through Talent category. His other writing credits include Gap Year, Cowards, Me and My Monsters, and Pandemonium, among others.

Talking about his theatre works, his 2010 play Party received positive reviews from The Guardian, which called it “an idiosyncratic and highly enjoyable piece performed beautifully by a crack cast of upcoming comics.” Tom was also longlisted for the stage adaptation of Franz Kafka’s The Trial called Joseph K. in the 2011 Evening Standard Theatre Awards Most Promising Playwright.
