The Origin Story

Hans Vestberg was born on June 23rd, 1965, in Hudiksvall, Sweden.

After completing his childhood schooling in Hudiksvall, where he was raised by his mother and father, he attended Uppsala University in Uppsala, Sweden.

He finished with a degree in Business Administration and Economics in 1991.

Career

@Getty

As the current CEO of Verizon, Hans Vestberg has had a long and distinguished career history.

After getting his college degree in 1991, he began his career in his hometown of Hudiksvall at Ericsson Cables.

He worked there for over 25 years, fulfilling various management roles for the company in Sweden, China, Brazil, Mexico, and the United States.

While in Brazil from 2000 to 2002, he worked as CFO for North America at Ericsson, later serving as president of the company in Mexico from 2002 to 2003.

He went on to work as senior vice president and executive vice president for Ericsson before becoming chief financial officer from 2007 to 2009.

In 2009, he was promoted to CEO, leading more than 115,000 employees in their shift to a modern business model.

Vestberg left the company for Verizon in 2016 following unfavorable results for that fiscal year.

Verizon

His first position with Verizon was chief technology officer and executive vice president of the network and technology team.

During his time in these roles, he oversaw the company’s fiber network and the development of its internet network.

In June of 2018, it was announced that he would take Lowell McAdam’s position as Verizon’s chief executive officer.

”I am humbled to be appointed CEO of Verizon at such an exciting and dynamic time for our company,” Vestberg said at the time.

”I am confident that Hans is the right person to bring Verizon through its next chapter,” McAdam said in a press release via Verizon.

Vestberg remains in his role as CEO of Verizon to this day.

Verizon is one of the world’s largest providers of mobile networks, the company accounting for over 35 percent of the world’s network infrastructure.

The company celebrated its twentieth-anniversary last year, having been founded in June of 2000.

In the fiscal year of 2020 alone, the company generated revenues of $128.3 billion. ”Right now in the U.S., Verizon customers are making 800 million calls a day,” Vestberg declared in April 2020 for HBR.

In 2021, Verizon’s revenue was $$133.6 billion.

Vestberg said that “2021 was a transformational year for Verizon that will serve as a catalyst for us.”
