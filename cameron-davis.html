Career

Cameron Davis was an exceptional amateur athlete. He won the Australian Amateur Championship in 2015. The same year, he finished second in the Asia-Pacific Amateur Championship, trailing only Jin Cheng. Similarly, he was a member of the Australian team that won the Eisenhower Trophy in Mexico. He won the solo tournament in 2016 by two strokes over fellow Australian Curtis Luck.

He turned pro the same year and finished tied for 15th place in the OHL Classic in Mayakoba. Similarly, after qualifying for the 2017 PGA Tour Canada by finishing fourth in Q-school, he tied for 14th in his first tournament, the Freedom 55 Financial Open, but went on to finish 76th in the tour’s standings and lose his card.

Furthermore, he won the Emirates Australian Open by a stroke over Jonas Blixt and Matt Jones in November, earning his first professional victory and automatic qualification into the 2018 Open Championship as part of the Open Qualifying Series.

In addition, he has Web.com Tour credentials for 2018, having advanced to the final round of Q School after starting at First Stage. Cameron Davis was also the winner of the Nashville Golf Open. He won his first PGA Tour title in a playoff at the Rocket Mortgage Classic on July 4, 2021, defeating Troy Merritt and Joaquin Niemann.
